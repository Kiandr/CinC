Sunday Symposium

The topic of this year's symposium will be Cardiology and Space Flight, where six exciting talks by renowned experts in the field will look at the heart and cardiovascular system in the space environment. The tentative program will be as follows: 
  • 13h00-13h45: Registration
  • 13h45-14h00: Opening remarks
  • 14h00-14h45: Dr. Robert Thirsk, Canadian Astronaut, Chancellor, University of Calgary, Calgary, Alberta, Canada Surviving and Thriving in Space - The Medical Consequences of Spaceflight 
  • 14h45-15h15: Dr. Peter Norsk, Chief Scientist at USRA, Element Scientist NASA-JSC Cardiovascular adaptation to spaceflight: Surprises and paradoxes
  • 15h15-15h45: Dr. Pierre-Francois Migeotte Three-Dimensional Ballistocardiography: from space research to clinical applications.
  • 15h45-16h30: Coffee break
  • 16h30-17h00: Dr. J. Kevin Shoemaker, University of Western Ontario, London, Ontario, Canada Simulated Microgravity and the Autonomic Nervous System
  • 17h00-17h30: Dr. Victor Convertino: The use of advanced machine learning to assess cardiovascular compensation in conditions of central hypovolemia: lessons learned from space and trauma. 
  • 17h30-18h00: Dr. Erik Seedhouse, Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University, Daytona, FL, USA. Application of bioinstrumentation in the development of a pressure suit for suborbital flight
  • 18h00-18h30: Closing remarks and discussion
  • 19h00-21h00: Reception at Marriott Pinnacle Hotel (Conference Hotel).
